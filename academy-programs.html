5-Star Sports Media Academy

Program Tracks

Six specialized paths. All built on real WNY sports coverage, real assignments, and real portfolio work. Choose a track — or talk to us about what fits you best.

Reporting Photography Broadcasting PR Digital Business
✍️
Track 1
Sports Reporting

The core craft of sports media. Students learn to cover games from start to finish — from the press box to the interview, from the first note to the published story. Every piece they write goes through an editorial process and is published under their byline on 5-Star Sports Media.

Students Learn
How to cover a game from start to finish
How to write a professional game recap
How to request and conduct athlete interviews
How to interview coaches after a game
How to write athlete spotlight features
How to write coach profile stories
How to fact-check names, scores, and stats
AP Style basics for sports writing
Track Assignments
2 game recaps (published)
1 athlete spotlight feature
1 coach interview story
📸
Track 2
Sports Photography

Visual storytelling is half the story. Photography students learn to capture the moments that define a game, a season, and a career — then present them professionally with the context and ethics that matter in sports media.

Students Learn
Game photography basics and positioning
Framing, focus, and action shot technique
Writing professional photo captions
Photo selection and editing workflow
Ethical photo use in sports media
Permission, media release, and credential basics
Phone photography for sports coverage
Gallery curation and presentation
Track Assignments
1 full game photo gallery (published)
1 athlete photo feature
1 coach or team photo set
🎙️
Track 3
Broadcasting & Interviews

The on-camera and on-mic skills that separate good communicators from great ones. Broadcasting students learn how to prepare for an interview, how to ask the right questions in the moment, and how to present themselves and their subjects with confidence and professionalism.

Students Learn
On-camera presence and confidence
Interview preparation and research
Question writing — follow-ups and open-ends
Athlete interview technique
Coach post-game interview protocol
Short-form video feature production
Podcast basics and audio quality
Professional presentation and tone
Track Assignments
1 athlete interview (recorded)
1 coach interview (recorded)
1 short feature segment
📣
Track 4
Public Relations & Sports Communications

The business of telling stories professionally. PR students learn how organizations, sponsors, and media work together — and how to communicate on behalf of a brand, team, or community figure in a way that builds trust and reputation.

Students Learn
Press release writing and format
Community storytelling and narrative
Sponsor communication and pitch basics
Writing organization spotlights
Media professionalism and ethics
Relationship building with media contacts
Crisis communication basics
Sports brand voice and tone
Track Assignments
1 organization spotlight story
1 sponsor/business feature draft
1 formal press release
📱
Track 5
Digital Content & Social Media

The fastest-growing lane in sports media. Digital students learn to create content that drives engagement, builds audiences, and tells sports stories in the formats modern fans actually consume — while learning the responsibility that comes with a platform and a following.

Students Learn
Social media captions for sports coverage
Short-form video planning and scripting
Building and managing a content calendar
Brand voice and audience consistency
Basic graphics and visual storytelling
Responsible posting and digital ethics
Platform strategy by audience type
Community engagement and growth
Track Assignments
5 published social posts
1 full content calendar
1 promotional campaign plan
💼
Track 6
Sports Business & Leadership

The big picture. Business and leadership students explore how sports create careers, communities, and economic opportunity — and learn to tell the stories of the people who built something beyond the game. This track is home to the Legends Spotlight and the "Beyond The Game" series.

Students Learn
How sports shape careers and communities
Sponsorship basics and value exchange
Personal branding through sports
Leadership storytelling and profiles
Sports entrepreneurship and opportunity
The "Beyond The Game" storytelling framework
Interviewing business and community leaders
Reflection writing and career vision
Track Assignments
1 Legends Spotlight draft
1 business owner interview
1 reflection essay — Beyond The Game
